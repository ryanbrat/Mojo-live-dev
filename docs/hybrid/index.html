Sport & Fitness (SF): Sport & Fitness--or Hybrid--bikes borrow varying aspects of both road & mountain bike geometries & componentries. As such, they exist on a spectrum with some bikes being "more road" on one side & some being "more mountain" at the opposing end of this continuum. Riders should pick bikes based upon their intended use. Depending on their place on the spectrum, they can be used for cross-training, trekking & commuting on a wide variety of surfaces, including: paved roads, unpaved roads, paths, & light trails. Variants such as the city bike, trekking bike, cross bike, comfort bike & commuter have differences that are specific to the riding styles they are built to support. Generally, however, SF bikes use a mountain bike's triple ring crank & its flat handlebars, giving riders a more upright & comfortable posture than road bicycles. SF bikes can feature rim or disc brakes, depending on what the rider needs. SF bikes often take the 700c (larger) road wheel diameter from road bikes. This provides less rolling resistance for higher speeds. However, they usually use a wider rim & tire for increased strength.

Cruiser: Cruiser bikes (also known as beach cruisers) feature large balloon tires, upright seating posture & flowing, expressive, styling. Cruisers are as much about style & comfort as they are about transportation. Most popular in the early 1930s through the 1950s, Cruisers have enjoyed renewed popularity since the late 1990s. New cruiser bike iterations have moved away from their single-speed heritage & now provide comfortable three speed, & even seven speed, options. These multi-speed options help them to be comfortable on longer rides. Their “retro” look, reliable mechanical performance, comfortable ride, & relatively low price make them a great option for folks who need a cycle for leisurely rides.
